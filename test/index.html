Here's How the Texas Power Crisis Unfolded

scroll to start

00_For two weeks in February, 2021, brutal winter storms caused Texans to be left without power for days on end.

01_During the crisis, all power-generation sources were negatively impacted by the cold weather with the most significant gap from coal and gas facilities.

02_To understand what caused the Electric Reliability Council of Texas to issue a level three emergency energy alert, let's dig into the gas supply and demand on one day: February 15.

03_Let’s start with the gas supply in Texas on February 15.

04_Using February 7 as a baseline, the normal gas supply is coming from Production (49%), Imports (13%), Storage withdrawals (38%).

05_On February 15, because of Production freeze-off (-20%), Import bottlenecks (-9%), Storage freeze-offs (-27%), the gas supply fell by approximately 56 percent.

06_Then, let’s focus on gas demand in Texas on February 15.

07_Nonessential liquefied natural gas (LNG) exports were cut first, with feed gas dropping by approximately 75 percent form Feb 7 to Feb 15, followed by net exports to other states.

08_Mexico exports were cut by one-third, even before the governor of Texas ordered cuts to exports.

09_Industrial activity dropped by about 40 percent, while residential and commercial customers, typically triaged first for gas service, were nonetheless cut by about 50 percent.

10_Facing both freeze-offs and a decline in fuel supply, gas power generation dropped about 40 percent.

11_Finally, the total gas demand on February 15 is 16 bcfd. Even if all power generation facilities had been weatherized, Texas supply was likely 6 bcfd short of demand needs.
